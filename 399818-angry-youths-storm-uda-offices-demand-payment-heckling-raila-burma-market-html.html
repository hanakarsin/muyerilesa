Angry youths storm UDA offices, demand payment for heckling Raila at Burma Market

Posted by Enda Jantzen on Monday, June 24, 2024

- The angry youths said they had not been paid despite doing the job of heckling Raila at Burma Market

- The charged youngsters flocked the UDA party premises, an outfit which is closely linked to DP Ruto

- However, Ruto's point man Dennis Itumbi denied claims the goons had been hired to frustrate the former prime minister

A group of angry youths has stormed United Democratic Alliance (UDA) party offices to demand payment for heckling former prime minister Raila Odinga.

The charged young men stormed the outfit's premises which is associated with Deputy President William Ruto and said they had done the job that they had been commissioned to do in frustrating Raila but had not received their wages.

In videos shared on social media platforms, the youngsters flocked the offices while insisting they would not leave without their payment.

"Yesterday we did our work and we have come here today to collect our payment," one of the men said.

Chants of "haki yetu" and "pesa yetu" (our right, out money) filled the air as the rowdy youths sang in unison and created a scene outside the offices whose gates remained shut despite the commotion.

However, Ruto's point man Dennis Itumbi denied claims that the goons had been hired by UDA and instead shifted the blame to MPs Babu Owino and Maina Kamanda.

"Kamanda and Junet Mohammed recall your goons. Ati goons at UDA offices, demanding their pay for telling Raila Odinga they support the #WheelbarrowMovement at Burma Market. Good people learn to embrace the truth," Itumbi said.

As reported by TUKO.co.ke, Raila and Nominated MP Maina Kamanda were met by a harsh crowd of traders during their stopover at Burma Market in Nairobi.

In videos shared on social media platforms on Wednesday, January 13, the ODM leader and the lawmaker were met with pro-Ruto and his hustler movement chants.

The Ruto supporters even had a wheelbarrow, which is linked to his hustler nation, and they raised it high in the air in an apparent dismissal of the former prime minister.

On his Twitter platform, Raila said he had made the stopover to say hello to traders at Burma Market.
