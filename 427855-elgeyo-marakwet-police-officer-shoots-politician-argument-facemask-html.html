Elgeyo Marakwet: Police Officer Shoots Politician During Argument Over Facemask

Posted by Enda Jantzen on Thursday, May 16, 2024
  • Barnaba Lagat, a one-time MCA in Elgeyo Marakwet, had confronted the police officers who assaulted a youngster for not having a facemask
  • The officers were reportedly involved in an altercation with Barnaba, during which one of them shot him in his arm
  • He was rushed to a health facility in Ziwa before being referred to Eldoret's Moi Referral and Teaching Hospital (MTRH)

A politician from Elgeyo Marakwet is nursing injuries after a police officer shot him on Tuesday, September 28.

He had intervened in an incident in which officers from Trans Nzoia cornered a youngster for allegedly flouting the anti-COVID-19 rules.

Identified as Barnaba Lagat, the politician, who once served as a ward representative, rushed to question the officers who were allegedly beating the youngster after he was found strolling at Kapsigilai without a facemask on.

The officers got angered by the confrontation instigated by Barnaba, one of them shooting him in his upper left arm before fleeing the scene using their van after the incident.

Barnaba was later rushed to Ziwa health centre before being referred to Moi Referral and Teaching Hospital (MTRH).

The area's residents condemned the act threatening to storm the officers' station in Kapchorop to protest the incident.

“How comes officers from another county could trespass to harass residents. We want the shooting incident investigated," said a local politician.

The area MP Joshua Kuttuny also condemned the act calling on the relevant agencies to pursue necessary action against the officers.

Barnaba was elected in 2013 to serve as the Kiptoror Ward MCA but lost the seat in 2017.

Trans Nzoia County Commander Jacinta Wesonga said the officers had crossed to Elgeyo Marakwet to conduct a crackdown on counterfeit goods in the area.

"The officers were conducting a raid on counterfeit goods when the public started to throwing stones at them, prompting the officers to shoot in the air," Wesonga said.

This is not the first (near)fatal incident involving police officers mandated to enforce COVID-19 protocols.

Police brutality in Kianjokoma

In August, protests erupted in Kianjokoma, Embu county when two young men died in police custody.

Benson Njiru and Emmanuel Mutura were apprehended for allegedly flouting curfew rules.

Njiru, a student at Kabarak University and Mutura who studied at Don Bosco Technology Training Institute, would later be found dead on Wednesday, August 4.

John Ndwiga Kamunyuti and Catherine Wawira accused the officers of killing their sons and dumping their bodies at Embu Level Five Hospital mortuary.
