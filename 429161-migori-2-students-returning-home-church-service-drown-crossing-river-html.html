Migori: 2 Students Returning Home from Church Service Drown while Crossing River

Posted by Lourie Helzer on Sunday, June 2, 2024
  • The students, who were in Form One and Form Four, were in the company of another young boy who survived
  • The survivor said he was the first to pass and advised the two not to cross as there was a rise in water levels but they ignored
  • Bodies of the deceased are yet to be retrieved from the river

Two male students from Mwache Secondary School have drowned in River Migori in Suna East.

In the Sunday, October 10, incident, the students were from a church event and drowned while trying to cross the river on their way back home.

Confirming the incident, Assistant Chief George Owiti said the deceased, who were in Form One and Form Four, were in the company of another young boy who survived.

As reported by Citizen, the boy, who safely managed to cross the river said he was the first to pass and advised the two not to cross as there was a surge in water levels.

Twins drown in Indian Ocean

In another incident, a family in Kwale was thrown into mourning after they lost their twin sons on Monday, May 31.

The duo identified as Solomon and Kennedy Gatindu were having a good time swimming at a beach in Gasi when they were swept by strong waves.

Their father Samuel Gatindu confirmed the tragic incident and revealed his sons had left the family farm whey they worked in the afternoon to go and unwind at the beach.

"We were with them at the farm about three o'clock. I was later informed they had drowned in the ocean," Samuel sadly said.

Student drowns while taking selfie

TUKO.co.ke earlier reported on an incident where a Form Two student in Gichugu, Kirinyaga county drowned after slipping into River Nyamindi.

Lucia Mwende is reported to have been taking selfies together with her friends when the tragedy occurred on Wednesday, December 16, 2020.

Lurencia Wambugi, the mother of the 15-year-old, said her daughter had left home with her three friends earlier in the day.
