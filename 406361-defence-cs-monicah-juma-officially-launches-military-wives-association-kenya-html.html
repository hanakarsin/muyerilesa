Defence CS Monicah Juma Officially Launches Military Wives Association of Kenya

Posted by Chauncey Koziol on Thursday, May 16, 2024

- Defence CS Monica Juma said the aim of the association was noble as it will promote the welfare of the families of KDF officers and service members

- According to her, the welfare of the troops was of utmost importance in the Ministry of Defence

- She further noted that a soldier performs best in his line of duty when he knows that his family back at home is taken care of

Monica Juma, Kenya's Cabinet Secretary for Defence, unveiled the Military Wives Association (MWAK) on Wednesday, March 17.

As a support system for families of the deceased, people with disabilities, retired and serving Kenya Defence Forces personnel, the association offers a forum to share ideas, connect and offer benefits to members (KDF).

The CS Defence praised the chairperson of MWAK, Tabitha Kibochi, and her team for conceiving and birthing the association.

She said its aim was noble as it will be promoting the welfare of the families of KDF officers and service members, whether serving, retired or deceased.

“The welfare of our troops is of utmost importance in the Ministry of Defence. It is known that a soldier performs best in his line of duty when he knows that his family back at home is taken care of," she said.

"I, therefore, have no doubt that the work and activities of the MWAK will support and augment the official efforts within the Ministry with regard to the welfare of families, children and widows of all ranks including those of retired personnel,” she added.

Chief of Defence Forces (CDF) General Robert Kibochi acknowledged the important role of soldiers' wives in caring for those facets of their lives that cannot be regimented within the direct military chain of command.

Tabitha Kibochi, the MWAK Chairperson, stated that the organization aims to strengthen KDF soldiers' welfare in a variety of fields, including maternal healthcare.

“We hope to achieve milestones in collaboration with the Compensation and Welfare department at the Defence Headquarters and additionally seek ideas from other charities for the benefit of our military families,” said Kibochi.

She shared her appreciation to the CDF and the entire KDF family for assisting the welfare department in fully implementing welfare services.

During the ceremony, instruments of authority were formally handed over to the association's leadership, mandating that they fully implement their activities.

The KDF Service Commanders, General Officers, Senior Officers, Officers, MWAK Members, and other guests attended the inauguration ceremony.
