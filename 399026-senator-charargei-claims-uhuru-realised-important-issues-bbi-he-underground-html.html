Nandi senator Samson Cherargei has claimed President Uhuru Kenyatta has realised Kenya has more important issues to deal with than the much-hyped Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) report.

Cherargei noted the president's silence on the subject was a clear message he was reconsidering his position and would most likely drop the agenda along the way.

Read the signs

Speaking to Citizen TV on Thursday, January 7, the lawmaker who is allied to Deputy President William Ruto's Tanga Tanga camp asked proponents of the BBI report particularly from the ODM wing to read the signs in advance.

"You can see President Kenyatta has gone underground because he has realised there are more important issues. As for the ODM leader, he is trying to flog a dead horse," he said.

He argued the handshake report was not only unpopular in Mt Kenya region but also across the country, adding it would fail if subjected to a referendum.

Cherargei's remarks came barely days after his Murang'a counterpart and Senate Majority Whip Irungu Kang'ata wrote a letter to the president informing him the BBI report he was vouching for was unpopular in his own Mt Kenya backyard.

The letter which has caused jitters in the ruling party has seen some political bigwigs from Central Kenya condemn Kang'ata for allegedly rocking the boat by misrepresenting facts.

Exit strategy

National Assembly Majority Leader Amos Kimunya claimed the Murang'a senator used the controversial letter as an exit plan to join Ruto's camp.

He accused the chief whip of whipping emotions to seek cheap political publicity at the expense of the unity of the country.

A host of governors from Mt Kenya region led by Kiraitu Murungi of Meru dismissed the content of Kang'ata's letter and reassured Uhuru Central Kenya was fully behind BBI.

However, a section of leaders from Uhuru's backyard and beyond have agreed with Kang'ata and asked the president to act accordingly.

Laikipia Woman Rep Cate Waruguru who has been supporting the report said the ground had shifted and it was necessary for Uhuru to take note.

Machakos Governor Alfred Mutua also concurred with the Murang'a senator's observation.

Misinformation

Speaking in the same interview with Cherargei, ODM secretary-general Edwin Sifuna said proponents of the BBI have not had enough time to tell Kenyans about the good things contained in the report.

He noted the resistance being witnessed was as a result of the perennial misinformation and misrepresentation of facts by those opposed to the document.
