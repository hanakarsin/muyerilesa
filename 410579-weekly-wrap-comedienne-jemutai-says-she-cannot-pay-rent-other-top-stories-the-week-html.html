Weekly Wrap: Comedienne Jemutai Says She Cannot Pay Rent, Other Top Stories of the Week

- Celebrated stand-up comedian, Stella Chemutai alias Jemutai asks for financial help to sustain her two babies.

- Anne Waiguru's son Ron Waiguru among students who participated in the African Schools Mooting Competition 2021 and won an award

This week saw different Kenyan celebrities and their young ones trend on social media for various reasons.

Here are seven stories that made it to the top of the trends.

Celebrated stand-up comedian, Stella Chemutai alias Jemutai, left many shocked after publicly asking for financial help to sustain her two babies.

Before her coming out, her story was trending after blogger Edgar Obare alleged that her kids' dad was fellow comedian Professor Hamo.

However, the mother of two talked to TUKO.co.ke and explained that things have been hard since COVID-10 hit, and booking gigs was a challenge for her, and she needed to find a way to pay rent and support the kids since her partner left her in 2020.

Comedian Otoyo celebrated his buddy Jalang’o’s birthday by sharing photos depicting their humble beginnings.

The two have been friends for years and have featured on numerous shows together and the two rose to fame after a duo match on the famous Papa Shirandula show.

From the photos, one could tell the two have come a long way, rising to become some of Kenya’s most successful entertainers.

An 85-year-old Indian man identified as Narayan Dabhalkar died on Monday, April 26, after he voluntarily gave up his hospital bed to a younger man.

His story went viral online as people praised him for sacrificing his much-needed bed after hearing the sick young man's wife pleading with the hospital to admit him.

Dabhabalkar chose to let him have his place and said he had lived his life and it was better for him to spend his last days with his family.

The COVID-19 death toll in India has seen crematoriums and graveyards increasingly becoming overwhelmed.

Officials in New Delhi said they were considering cutting down more trees for the burial rites.

A gravedigger said he was afraid they could run out of space to bury the high number of people who are dying due to the ongoing global disease.

Kirinyaga County Governor Anne Waiguru's son Ron Waiguru was among news-makers this week. He was among the students who participated in the African Schools Mooting Competition 2021 at the University of Dundee.

The prestigious competition featured students from Ghana, Kenya, Nigeria, Uganda, and Zimbabwe.

Thirteen teams competed against an opposing lawyer and in front of two judges and the team from Kenya's Brookhouse School emerged the best in the competition.

Gospel singer Size 8's daughter excited fans online after her parents shared her perfect pronunciation of the word thirsty.
