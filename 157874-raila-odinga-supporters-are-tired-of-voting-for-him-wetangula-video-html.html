Moses Wetangula's bitter truth after Raila's western tour

Posted by Enda Jantzen on Sunday, April 21, 2024

- CORD co-principal Moses Wetangula has dismissed party leader Raila Odinga's ongoing Western tour

- Bungoma Senator says Raila Odinga should not vie for the presidency in 2017 but instead let him run

- He also dismissed the large crowds witnessed during rallies held in Western Kenya by Raila Odinga

Moses Wetangula on Thursday night, July 21, asked his co-principal Raila Odinga to step down from his presidential bid saying there are slim chances of him winning in the elections.

Wetangula said that Odinga has vied for the presidency three times and failed, he should, therefore, give the opportunity to him or to Wiper leader Kalonzo Musyoka.

He further reminded his co-principal that the large crowds witnessed during rallies held in Western Kenya will not translate into votes.

Wetangula said he believes the CORD coalition has the capacity to win 2017 elections but asked Raila to consider that large crowds do not translate into votes.

He claimed that Odinga's voters are tired of voting for him only for the votes to be rigged, therefore urging him to allow fresh people in the race

The Bungoma senator maintains that CORD will not win the 2017 elections if Raila Odinga runs as the presidential candidate.

Wetangula launched his presidential bid on April 2, 2016, which Raila Odinga skipped.

The bid which was marred by violence raised eyebrows within the political circles many foreseeing trouble within the coalition.

Raila Odinga is on a tour in Western Kenya meeting with ODM voters in a bid to quell the growing rebellion among Western Kenya leaders.

Budalangi MP Ababu Namwamba, Funyula MP Paul Otuoma and Sirisia’s John Walukhe have already resigned from their top positions in the party.
