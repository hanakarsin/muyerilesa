Homa Bay: Family in Distress after Learning Their Son Went Missing in August, Never Reported to Scho

  • The distraught parents blamed the school administration for what they termed as sheer negligence

A family in Homa Bay county is distraught after their son went missing upon returning for the first-term half break.

Franklin Otieno Otieno had left home to return to Homa Bay High School, but records at the institution showed he never reported back on Monday, August 30 as was expected.

"Franklin Otieno Otieno of Form Three Blue did not report to school so according to the records, he has been absent since the time he left for half term break," insisted Vincent Mayienga who spoke to NTV Kenya on Friday, October 2.

His father Andrew Ogwang' said he came to realise his son had been missing when he sent him transport money as schools were breaking for the end of the first term.

Ogwang' was taken aback when Otieno's class teacher demanded to know if indeed he was the father yet the school administration had all his details.

"The schools were closing this week and so I sent fare to the class teacher, he called and asked me if I am the father of Otieno. I asked him why he has been looking for me yet they have my contact in the registration form since I am the one who took him to school when he joined Form One," he said.

The distraught parents blamed the school administration for what they termed as sheer negligence and failing to inform him when they discovered the student had not reported.

"To me, this is sheer negligence on behalf of the school administration because under the COVID-19 protocols, we are not allowed to come and visit our children so we expect the teachers to inform us of the welfare of our children and that is WhatsApp groups are there," he added.

The matter was reported to Oyugis police station where the family filed a missing person report as the search for the juvenile continued.

"We reported the matter to Oyugis police station and we have an OB number," Ogwang' disclosed.

Missing student found safe

Early in August, a 14-year-old Nairobi girl who went missing on her way to school was found in Narok.

Marion Wangu Mukura, a pupil at Reby-Peter Memorial Academy at Kahawa West, is said to have never returned home on Friday, July 30, after she left in the morning for school.
