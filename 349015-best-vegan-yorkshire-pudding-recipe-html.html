Best vegan Yorkshire pudding recipe

Posted by Enda Jantzen on Monday, January 1, 0001

Yorkshire pudding is a popular side dish originating from England. It is a multipurpose food that can be served in various ways based on ingredient choices, pudding size, and the complementary components of the meal. If eaten as a first course, Yorkshire pudding can be enjoyed with onion gravy. Vegetarians have no reason to worry since the best vegan Yorkshire pudding recipe does not have animal products.

Although vegan Yorkshire pudding is a component of the traditional Sunday roast, it may be topped up with foods like mash and bangers to make a complete meal. Traditional puddings cannot be considered vegan since they include milk and eggs. The milk component can be replaced with any milk from plants, but the eggs are not easy to substitute.

The eggs play a unique role when it comes to making delicious Yorkshire puddings. Other types of vegan baking methods that include chia eggs or a couple of flax as an egg replacer can produce similar results as regular eggs. Thus, it is possible to make tasty vegan Yorkshire pudding as shown in the simplified recipe below.

Vegan Yorkshire pudding recipe - What you'll need

The following are the ingredients you will need for the simple vegan Yorkshire pudding recipe:

  • 25 grams of flour
  • 100 grams of self-raising flour
  • 2 tablespoons of sunflower oil
  • 100 millilitres of juice from chickpeas
  • 250 millilitres unsweetened milk substitute like diary-free almond milk
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder

What makes a Yorkshire rise?

Yorkshire puddings rise because of the rapid cooking of the flour and butter mixture producing steam. Hence, the process requires a high-heat oven and hot oil before pouring the batter into the tin of Yorkshire pudding. Once the vegan Yorkshire pudding has risen and almost complete, you can take it to the lower shelf of the oven to finish cooking.

How to prepare the best vegan Yorkshire puddings

Here is a simplified step-by-step vegan Yorkshire pudding recipe

  • Heat the oven first to 220C/gas mark 7.
  • Divide the oil into a 6-hole muffin pan then put it in the oven.
  • While the oil heats, sift the gram flour, baking powder and flour into a large bowl. Add a little fine ground pepper and a pinch of salt.
  • Mix the milk alternative, and chickpea juice then beat into the dry ingredients until smooth. Pour the mixture into a large jug.
  • Before you place it in the oven, pour the batter into the muffin pan containing the oil then bake for 10 minutes.
  • Turn down the oven 190C/gas mark 5 without opening the door. Bake the pudding for 15 more minutes until it becomes golden brown, puffed up, crisp and thoroughly cooked.
  • Serve the vegan Yorkshire pudding immediately. You can also cool and freeze it for up to one month.

    • If you have enough time, it is better to make the batter at least one hour in advance and allow it to rest before baking. Note also that the traditional Yorkshire pudding pan may not be appropriate since it is shallow. So, it is advisable to use popover pan or muffin pan for Yorkshire pudding recipe vegan since the two options are deep.

    How do you make a hole in vegan Yorkshire puddings?

    To make a hole when following the above vegan Yorkshire pudding recipe, pour a teaspoon of oil into each 12-bun tray hole, or 3 tablespoons into roasting pan, or a tablespoon into each hole of a 4-hole tin. Next, place the puddings in the oven until the oil is piping hot. After about five minutes, remove it carefully from the oven and pour batter into the tin or holes in equal measure.

    Preparing puddings free of animal products has never been this easy with the above vegan Yorkshire pudding recipe. The appearance and taste are still like the traditional Yorkshire puddings. You can serve it with your favourite drink, veggies, or roasted potatoes.
