How to Make Liquid Soap – Simple Life Hacks You Need to Know

Very few people are aware of how to make a liquid soap. Before getting to it, however, you need to understand that it may not be as simple as the cold hard pressing procedure you could be used to. Liquid soap making is more complicated and requires that you exercise a lot of precision to achieve results. Instead of sodium hydroxide that used in bar soaps, liquid soaps will require you to use potassium hydroxide to saponify the liquid soap.

Before you learn the steps on how to make liquid soap in Kenya, you need to have the following essential ingredients.

  • 500mls of sodium lauryl ether sulfate
  • 500g of sodium chloride /normal salt or iodized salt
  • 500mls of cocodiethanolamide
  • 20gms of a water-soluble dye
  • 40mls of benzalkonium chloride
  • 20mls of fragrance to Perfume the soap
  • 20 liters of distilled water
  • Medium sized basin
  • 500ml pet bottles for packaging and storing the final product
  • Gas stove to boil the water
  • Pot for boiling water
  • Measuring pots and cups to measure water and ingredients
  • Plastic ladle to mix

How to Make Liquid Soap at Home

    Important Things to Remember

    If you are making liquid bathing soap for the first time, it’s advisable that you begin with the simple recipes. Most of the liquid bathing soaps that are made without water may be used for a few months. However, those that use water or coconut milk are more perishable as they may harbor bacteria that may render the mixture unsafe. Finally, you may discover that your homemade liquid bathing soap won’t lather. However, this doesn’t mean that your soap doesn’t work. You can add more glycerin if you want to achieve sudsy lather.
