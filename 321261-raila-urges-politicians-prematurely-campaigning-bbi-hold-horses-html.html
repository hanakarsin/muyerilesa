Raila urges politicians prematurely campaigning against BBI to hold their horses

Posted by Lourie Helzer on Monday, January 1, 0001

- The Opposition doyen said handshake team was yet to table its recommendations to Kenyans

- He said leaders should not oppose what they don't know about and asked for more patience

- He said the BBI team was solely formed to help solve tribalism, corruption and electoral injustices

- The team is expected to table the report to President Uhuru Kenyatta and Raila Odinga

Opposition leader Raila Odinga has told off Jubilee politicians campaigning against Constitution amendment proposals by the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) team saying nobody knows what the team has at hand.

The political heavyweight said leaders should stop debating the team's proposals and wait until it presents the report for them to look at and recommend any necessary changes.

Raila was speaking on Saturday, October 26, at Pendukiani in Kilifi county during the burial of Jola Ngumbao, uncle of Ganda MCA-elect Reuben Katana, who was shot dead in chaos involving Malindi MP Aisha Jumwa and ODM supporters in the ward.

"Surprisingly, there are some leaders who have started to oppose the BBI but do not know what they are opposing because the report has not yet been submitted. I urge Kenyans to stay calm until the report is ready," he said.

The former prime minister noted he and Uhuru had resolved to come up with the BBI for three key reasons, that is, to end tribalism, end election rigging and fight the scourge of corruption.

The task force is expected to hand over the report to the two leaders soon.

He emphasized elections should not be an issue of live and death and that those involved the murder of Ngumbalo be investigated and brought to justice.

"After the last general election, I reconciled with President Uhuru and we declared that we would not allow anyone to die in this country due to politics or elections. It is very sad that the deceased was shot dead in his home," he said.

Already, over 39 Jubilee legislators from Mt Kenya have vowed to oppose the BBI report should it fail to address issues affecting their constituents.

In a joint statement read by Ndaragwa MP Jeremiah Kioni on Thursday, October 24, at Parliament Buildings, the lawmakers said they will not accept anything short of equality in representation and empowerment of the people they represent.

The BBI proposals are likely to raise political temperatures in the country ahead of the 2022 general election.
