NTSA dismisses reports it's going back on Kenyan roads due to increased accidents

Posted by Enda Jantzen on Tuesday, August 20, 2024

The National Transport and Safety Authority (NTSA) has dismissed reports by a section of the media that it has returned to the roads in the wake of increased accidents across the country.

The Authority on Wednesday, October 2, clarified it had not received any order or circular from Interior Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiang'i directing that it returns to the roads, contrary to the reports doing rounds on social media.

In a statement sent to newsrooms, NTSA which was pulled out of all roads following a directive by President Uhuru Kenyatta in January 2019, noted it will nevertheless continue with its nationwide road safety sensitisation campaigns to increase public awareness.

"Our attention is drawn to an article regarding an order by the Cabinet Secretary, Dr. Fred Matiang'i that NTSA returns to the road after a spike in crashes. The Authority wishes to clarify that no circular has been issued to that effect contrary to article. NTSA will not be going back on the roads to undertake any enforcement activities," the statement read.

The regular further noted it will also continue to provide the necessary support to the National Police Service to effectively enforce traffic rules.

Reports had emerged earlier in the day indicating NTSA had taken over from the traffic police after its officers were spotted conducting impromptu checks on motor vehicles along the Nyeri-Nairobi highway amid intensified crackdown.

Meanwhile, the Authority has directed Nairobi county government to cancel seasonal parking tickets for nine matatu Saccos whose vehicles were suspended on Monday, September 30.

"This is to notify you that the following Saccos have been suspended by the NTSA for failing to comply with traffic regulations. You are hereby advised to suspend any further issuance of seasonal ticketing," the transport regulator said.

Below is a list of affected Saccos:

1. City Tram Sacco ( City Center-Jogoo Road-Buruburu-Outering-Umoja route).

2. Mwamba Sacco (City center-Buruburu-Civil Servant-Komarock route).

3. Marvellous Travellers (Komarock-Savanna-Jogoo Road-Eastleigh-City Center route).

4. MNGN Sacco (Mathare North-Ngara-Gikomba route).

5. Fig Komba (Dandora-Juja Road-Gikomba/Fig Tree-Ngara-Gikomba-Kiambio/Mathare North-Ngara-Gikomba-Fig tree-Kiambio route).

6. Moonlight Coach Company (Commercial-Thika Road-Mathare North route).

7. Mwirona Sacco (Bus Station-Thika Road-Kasarani-Mwiki route).

8. Huruma Mini Bus (Huruma-Pangani-Kariokor-Tusker route).

9. Transafaris (Nairobi-Nakuru-Kisumu route).

The Authority accused the blacklisted Saccos of failing to comply with traffic regulations which require one Sacco to register not more than 30 public service vehicles.
