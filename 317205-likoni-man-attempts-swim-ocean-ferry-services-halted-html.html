Likoni man attempts to swim across ocean after ferry services were halted

Posted by Chauncey Koziol on Wednesday, June 26, 2024

There was panic at Likoni channel when a man attempted to swim across the ocean after ferry services were suspended during search of missing bodies on Wednesday, October 2.

The man simply identified as Timiza left members of the public and Likoni ferry management in awe by his adventurous courage oblivious of potential danger that lurked around.

Despite being disabled with only one functional right leg, the slender middle-aged Timiza swam half way prompting Kenya Rescue Divers to block his way.

The rescuers were in a mission to find bodies of Mariam Kigenda and her daughter Amanda, who drowned after a car they were traveling in fell off the ferry on Sunday, September 29.

Timiza was arrested and taken away.

In a related story, Kenya Ferry Service recovery teams resumed a multi-agency search in the ocean for the two bodies.

Experts combed the nine mapped points where it is believed the vehicle and its two occupants lie.

Story by Yvonne Mutunga, TUKO.co.ke correspondent in Mombasa county.
