Tongue-Tied Babies: Mom Narrates Dreadful Breastfeeding Experience, Says It Damaged Her Breasts

Posted by Hettie Henneman on Sunday, July 28, 2024
  • When her son was born, Lilian Wanjau did not immediately notice that he had a condition known as tongue-tie
  • This caused excruciating pain to the mother when breastfeeding
  • The child had to undergo surgery to correct the condition

Lilian Wanjau remembers when her son was born, she did not immediately notice that he had a tongue-tie.

Lilian started breastfeeding her child and all went well until a few days into it.

She started to feel a lot of pain when the baby was breastfeeding.

"So I went for my two weeks checkup . I told my ob-gyn that something was not right. She told me to consult a lactation consultant to check on us. I went to two visits with the lactation consultant and explained to them how painful breastfeeding was and my nipples were cut and bleeding," Wanjau told TUKO.co.ke.

The lactation consultant then suggested that Wanjau uses a nipple shield and try changing the positioning of the baby when feeding him. They even gave her a hospital grade breast pump. But the pain did not stop.

"I dreaded each breastfeeding session. I noted even the pumping was making the situation worse so I returned the breast pump they gave me. I decided to get a second opinion from a government agency that helps mothers and children here in the USA. When the lactation consultant there checked on my son she noted he had a tongue tie," Wanjau told TUKO.co.ke.

Wanjau had no idea what that was. It was then that the doctor told her that this should have been checked the minute she started complaining of pain when breastfeeding.

"I was told to contact my son's paediatrician and he was scheduled for outpatient surgery to correct the tongue-tie. I was referred back to the hospital lactation consultants and when they saw the damage on my nipples they advised me to stop breastfeeding. That was the end of our breastfeeding journey. My son was four months," Wanjau narrated.

For her second child, Wanjau was now prepared and armed with information.

"As soon as she was delivered I asked the doctors to check if she had a tongue tie. Unfortunately, 6 hours after birth they had to rush her to the Nicu. She was on machines for 2 weeks and I was not allowed to breastfeed her. The nurses encouraged me to continue pumping so that my breast milk would not disappear," she recalled.

When she was finally discharged, Wanjau tried breastfeeding and her daughter was not a fan.

"She would get very frustrated and cry because of not having the instant milk she was used to from a bottle. Contacted lactation consultants and they advised as long as she was feeding from the bottle, all that mattered was to pump and feed her instead. That is what I resulted to do until she was eight months," Wanjau explained.

She swore never to judge any parent that does not breastfeed a child because it is not a natural process for all.
