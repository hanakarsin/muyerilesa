What are the minimum entry requirements?

The Kenya Police recruitment announcement customarily comes with the minimum requirements for any individual to join the force. Therefore, one ought to meet the conditions to be considered an eligible candidate. What are the requirements for the 2021 recruitment drive?

Recruitment of police constables

If you are looking to partake in the National Police Service recruitment exercise and join as a police constable, you ought to abide by the following minimum entry requirements:

  • Be a Kenyan citizen holding a valid Kenyan National Identity card.
  • Possess a minimum mean grade of D+ (D Plus) and above in the Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) examination or its equivalent from an examination body recognized in Kenya with a D+ (D Plus) and above in either English or Kiswahili languages.
  • Be aged between 18 and 28 years.
  • Be physically and medically fit.
  • Meet the requirements of Chapter Six of the Constitution of Kenya.
  • Have no criminal record or any pending criminal charges.

Another vital thing to keep in mind when assessing the Kenya Police Recruitment requirements is that pregnant women cannot partake in the exercise. This also applies to pregnancies that occur during the entire training period.

The National Police Service doesn't accept results slips, and applicants shouldn't present any false information. Engaging in any corrupt activity or canvassing might lead to disqualification.

Recruitment of cadet inspectors

The National Police Service also recruits cadets during its nationwide recruitment drive. If you are looking to fill this position, you ought to meet the following requirements:

  • Be a citizen of Kenya and hold a Kenya National Identity Card.
  • Must be a holder of at least an undergraduate degree from a recognized university/institution and a minimum mean grade of C+ (Plus) with C+ (plus) in English or Kiswahili in the Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) examination.
  • Be aged between 22 and 30 years.
  • Must be registered with the relevant statutory body, where applicable.
  • Meet the requirements of Chapter Six of the Constitution.
  • Be physically and medically fit.
  • Have no criminal record or pending criminal charges.

Kenya Police recruitment dates in 2021

The National Police Service ordinarily recruits cadet inspectors and constables with different dates. If you were interested in joining the police service as a constable, then the recruitment process's date was on February 22, 2021, from 8:00 am to 5:00 pm at the listed constituency-based recruitment centres.

People looking to become part of the police service as cadet inspectors aren’t required to visit any recruitment centres but submit their applications through an online portal. The application dates for cadet inspectors was between February 2, 2021, and February 15, 2021.

Application forms

Interested applicants ought to fill the prerequisite application form when going for the force's recruitment. Fortunately, with advancements in technology, people don't have to queue in line for the documents but can download them from the official National Police Service website.

The form possesses five sections including:

  • Personal details
  • Educational background
  • Employment history
  • Disciplinary actions or convictions
  • Medical history
  • Referees
  • Additional information

The last page of the application form has a declaration that you must agree to before submitting it to the recruiter.

You are also required to attach a copy of your national identification card, a copy of your secondary school leaving certificate, a copy of your KCSE certificate and birth certificate. Candidates are then asked to fill the forms and report with them during the recruitment day.

Kenya police recruitment procedure

Getting into the Kenya police is not a one-off activity. You have to go through various recruitment stages before being confirmed as an officer of the Kenya Police.

Screening and examination

This cannot commence unless the application deadline has elapsed. The employer will then scrutinize the applications and select successful applicants to proceed to the next step.

They look at things that would disqualify you, and only the most suitable is selected. The successful candidates are then invited for screening and examination through email.

Recruitment and training

Once you have passed screening, you will proceed to the Kenya police recruitment training. It is a rigorous exercise that involves physical drilling and a course on the Kenya police's core values.

Recruitment pass-out

It is the hallmark of GSU recruitment. Upon completing training, the candidates prove that they are ready and fit to join the Kenya police. They are thus recruited into the police based on their ranks.

What can disqualify you?

Prospective candidates looking to join the National Police Service should not jeopardize their integrity by doing any of the following things because it will lead to automatic disqualification:

  • Please do not take your result slips during the application because they are not accepted.
  • Canvassing either directly or indirectly for any position will also lead to disqualification.
  • The use of false documents is not only discouraged, but it will also lead to the criminal prosecution of the candidate in question.
  • Based on the provisions of section 25 of the National Police Service Commission Act, any applicant who lies during the recruitment process is bound to be arrested and jailed for a term not more than two years or fined Ksh. 200,000.
  • Do not offer the recruiting officers any form of gifts or gratification because it will only lead to disqualification.
  • Kenya police do not take corruption cases lightly, so if you are caught lobbying by offering money, you will be disqualified and prosecuted.

Constituency-based recruitment centres

CID Kenya recruitment that happens via the general police recruitment announced by the National Police Service occurs at various centres for constables. For instance, Mombasa County has seven recruitment centres distributed in its constituencies and sub-counties.

The same applies to the other 46 counties, but the number of recruitment centres might vary. Counties have more than one recruitment centre for logistical reasons and make sure that every interested applicant gets an opportunity to participate in the recruitment drive.

The Kenya Police Recruitment drive affords individuals interested in joining the force an opportunity to serve the community in various ways. Therefore, put your documentation in good order and check out the qualifications before going to the recruitment centres to become part of the National Police Service.
