Exiled Kenyan lawyer Miguna Miguna has expressed his joy after winning a racism case at the Court of Appeal in Ontario, Canada.

In a statement issued on his social media pages, the combative attorney shared the detailed judgment made by the Appellate Court which ruled in his client's favour.

Miguna was acting on behalf of a black Canadian who faced imminent eviction from his landlords identified as Benjamin Salsberg and Bruce E. Bussin.

"The Court of Appeal has affirmed our victory. Chief Justice Strathy, Justice Feldman and Justice Sossin have held that Morgan J. was entitled to conclude that racist landlords cannot use excuses to evict African/black businesses. Viva!" Miguna tweeted.

Case hearing

The said landlords wanted to kick out the respondent over what they termed as failure to give timely written notice of intention to exercise an option to renew the lease.

According to the appellants, the tenant took an assignment of the lease on July 30, 2013, for a five-year renewable term.

On expiry, the tenant, however, did not give a notice of extension making the landlord to keep him as an overholding tenant from August 2, 2017, to May 28, 2020, when they terminated the lease.

At the hearing of the application, the landlord also submitted that the tenant was “unattractive”, and his business did not attract “family-oriented customers”.

Miguna's client operated a restaurant that served African and Caribbean food.

The tenant in his submission through Miguna said his attempts to contact the landlord and the property manager were futile as his calls were not answered as both wanted him out.

In the ruling, the judges found the tenant acted in good faith and had an impressive rent payment record.

"The tenant was not in breach of the lease; its business was successful, it had never missed a rent payment and it continued to pay rent during the COVID-19 pandemic while operating a take-out service. The Tenant had made substantial investments in the premises, installing approximately $150,000 (KSh 16.1 million) worth of improvements.

Miguna who doubles up as a solicitor based in Toronto, Canada, shared his recent photo working in his office on Tuesday, May 18.
