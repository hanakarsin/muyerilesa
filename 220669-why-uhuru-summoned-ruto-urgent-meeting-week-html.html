Why Friday, November 4 is such an important day for Jubilee

Posted by Chauncey Koziol on Saturday, February 3, 2024

- The newly-formed Jubilee party will on Friday, November 4, move to quell internal fighting in the party by revealing the party's leadership

- The move had been delayed since the party's leadership has been out of the country on foreign trips

After months of waiting, the Jubilee Party has finally announced that it will be naming its party's interim officials on Friday, November 4.

The announcement on Tuesday, November 1 revealed that the party would hold a special National Governing Council (NGC) to quell power struggles within the party.

A number of high-ranking politicians who had joined the outfit had earlier complained that the top brass had gone quiet after the party's launch at Kasarani.

Internal wrangles at the party caused by the fear that some leaders would lose out in party nominations didn't help matters with part of the leaders saying that they regretted joining the party in the first place.

According to the Jubilee Party constitution, the NDC is the second in power organ at the party. Uhuru is the party leader while DP Ruto is his deputy.

Leader of Majority in parliament, Aden Duale had earlier stated that the party had failed to announce its leaders earlier because the top brass had been in and out of the country on foreign trips.

Uhuru and Ruto apparently met last week to deliberate on the leaders who would steer the party.

The JP so far has 31 party positions both at the national and county levels. The national will have 21 positions while 10 positions will be at the county level.

The party which was launched on Saturday, September 10, saw a merger of 12 parties form one of Kenya's biggest political alliance ahead of the 2017 General Election.
