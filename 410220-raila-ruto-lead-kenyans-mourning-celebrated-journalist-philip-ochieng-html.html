Deputy President William Ruto and Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) party leader Raila Odinga are among senior political leaders who have mourned celebrated journalist Philip Ochieng'.

Ochieng, a veteran journalist, and author passed away on the evening of Tuesday, April 27, at the age of 82.

His family members said that he succumbed to pneumonia and old age complications at his Awendo home in Migori County.

He had just been discharged from Ombo mission hospital a week earlier.

Ruto mourned him as a consummate, no-nonsense, and committed journalist whose depth of knowledge and understanding of issues was exceptional.

"The late Philip will without no doubt be remembered for his grammatical accuracy. He used his print media platforms to break down the English language and teach us new things even those we figured we knew," said Ruto.

Ruto said that Ochieng's readers will miss his illuminating columns.

"May his rich legacy endure forever. Our love and prayers to the Ochieng’ family, their loved ones, and the media fraternity. Rest In Peace," Ruto wrote on his official Twitter handle.

Raila said that the country had lost a veteran journalist and legendary columnist who used the power of the pen to communicate to his readers.

"Kenya has indeed lost in the passing of Philip Ochieng, a veteran journalist and legendary columnist. Through the power of the pen, the late Philip spoke to the most powerful and moved the society into action," said Raila in a statement.

Kenya National Qualifications Authority (KNQA) CEO Juma Mukhwana termed the late Ochieng' as a rare gem in the world of journalism.

"He was a fine brain that brilliantly guarded humanity against injustices. He used his pen to change the world. We have lost a walking encyclopedia, a human dictionary who knew all English words, a king of grammar, a mentor who raised accomplished journalists," said Mukhwana.

According to his biography book dubbed 'Fifth Columnist, a Legendary Journalist', the writer Liz Gitonga-Wanjohi described him as a person who beat many odds to become one of the most celebrated media personalities of his time.

While working at Nation Media Group, Ochieng rose from an intern to the editor-in-chief position in the company.
