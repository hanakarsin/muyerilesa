Raila says Joho has right to contest for presidential ticket: &quot;ODM is a democratic party&quot;

Posted by Chauncey Koziol on Sunday, August 4, 2024

- Raila Odinga said he is fine with Hassana Joho pursuing his presidential ambition under ODM

- He said his party provides equal chances for anyone who wants to vie for any political seat

- Joho has been conveying his thirst for the presidency insisting the former prime minister should reciprocate the support he has shown him for years

The Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) leader Raila Odinga has said Mombasa Governor Hassan Joho has a right to contest for a presidential ticket under his party.

The Mombasa governor has been saying he will run for president in the 2022 elections insisting he is the best-suited candidate in ODM to succeed President Uhuru Kenyatta.

Speaking on Tuesday, March 2, Raila said his Opposition outfit is a democratic party and anyone can run for any seat they feel they can handle.

"It is very okay for him to run for president because ODM is a Democratic party. Every five years, the country goes for elections; and ODM being a national party, everyone has the chance to run for the political seats that they want," he said.

In January 2021, the ODM) deputy party leader said he is ripe for the country's top job and has enough experience to steer the nation forward.

The politician nicknamed the Sultan argued he had supported the former prime minister for a long time and as such, it was time for him to reciprocate.

"I have stood with ODM for a long period of time. I have supported Raila Odinga for long and I want to send a message out for them to understand that I am now in the race to State House using the ODM ticket," Joho declared.

Nevertheless, though Raila is yet to publicly declare whether he will on the ballot in 2022, his close allies have been insisting he will also throw his hat in the ring.

As TUKO.co.ke reported, ODM chairman John Mbadi said his party leader will be on the ballot.

The Suba South Member of Parliament (MP) revealed the Opposition supremo will be in the State House race adding that Raila's diary will be busier in the coming days as he is expected to run his 2022 project as he attempts to take another stab at the top seat which has eluded him a record four times.

“We have started rolling out activities. Anyone who thinks we are not in this thing (presidential race) should know we are there,” Mbadi said in January 2021.
