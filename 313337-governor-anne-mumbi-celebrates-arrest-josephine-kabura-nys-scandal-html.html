Kirinyaga Governor Anne Mumbi celebrates arrest, charging of Josephine Kabura over NYS scandal

Posted by Lourie Helzer on Thursday, June 6, 2024

- Business woman Josephine Kabura and others are facing money laundering charges

- The accused reportedly facilitated transfer of millions of shilling in between banks accounts

- The former salonist was named as key suspect in KSh 971 million NYS scandal

Kirinyaga Governor Anne Mumbi is over the moon following arrest of former salonist and business woman Josephine Kabura over National Youth Service (NYS) phase one scandal.

Mumbi who had been cleared of any wrongdoing in connection to the NYS saga said arrest and arraignment of Kabura was a welcome move that was long overdue.

"‪This took a while, it’s been a long time coming but here it is! May justice be our shield and defender. Still waiting for her to be charged for swearing a false affidavit and charges against her promoters," the governor posted on Tuesday, August 19.

Kabura who appeared alongside nine other suspects including Ben Gethi, faced fresh charges of money laundering that taxpayers lose a total KSh 791 million between 2014 and 2015.

The suspects's lawyers poked holes in prosecution evidence produced before Nairobi senior principal magistrate Martha Mutuku, with each seeking to convince the court.

Throughout the proceedings, prosecutors held that there were enough pieces of evidence linking the accused to illegal transfer of the looted money.

Part of the loot was reportedly used to purchase cars, pieces of land and houses even though the accused were aware it was proceeds of corruption.

In October 2018, the Director of Public Prosecution Noordin Haji recommended fresh investigations into mysterious loss of KSh 791 million from the youth institute.

Haji confirmed Kabura lied in an affidavit she filed in 2015 where the business woman alleged she was working at the behest of Mumbi, the then the Devolution Cabinet secretary.
