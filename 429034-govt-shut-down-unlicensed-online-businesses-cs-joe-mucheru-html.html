Gov't to Shut Down Unlicensed Online Businesses, CS Joe Mucheru

Posted by Lourie Helzer on Wednesday, July 17, 2024
  • Communications Authority of Kenya noted the e-commerce sector has been penetrated by many service providers
  • The authority will carry out a scoping study to ascertain the size and growth of e-commerce in the country
  • ICT Cabinet Secretary Mucheru also stressed the need to regulate the sector for seamless growth and transparency

The Communication Authority of Kenya (ICT) is planning to shut down unlicensed postal and courier service providers to rid the sector of unscrupulous businessmen.

The authority's new Director-General Ezra Chiloba said the e-commerce sector has been penetrated by many service providers that have come with some challenges in terms of regulation.

Speaking at Stanley Hotel in Nairobi on Friday, October 8, he said his team would first carry out a scoping study to ascertain the size and growth of e-commerce in the country.

“The authority is currently carrying out a market study in the postal and courier sub-sector in order to establish the service access gaps to inform remedies and initiatives towards boosting services for consumers,” he stated.

ICT Cabinet Secretary Joe Mucheru who also attended the World Post Day stressed the need to regulate the sector for seamless growth and transparency.

He echoed Chiloba's sentiments disclosing that the sector recorded high growth in 2020 due to COVID-19 restrictions.

“My ministry and the industry regulator noted with concern that a number of firms took advantage of the COVID-19 pandemic to start offering unlicensed courier services in the country. I am calling upon the CA to enhance its enforcement interventions across the country and ensure those firms operating without licences are weeded out of the market," Mucheru said.

Mails by drones

Elsewhere, Kenya Postal Corporation has partnered with Swift Lab Limited, to roll out drone-based Last-Mile Delivery Services countrywide for medical supplies, e-commerce purchases and essential cargo.

Under the three-year agreement, Swift Lab Limited, a Kenyan cargo drone company, will manufacture and operate drones that can travel up to 70km and carry a payload of up to 4kg.

“We want to invest in the production of drones, it is not only our obligation as government to invest in Kenyan technology, but we are also keen on clawing back our market position, as the courier and logistics services provider of choice,” said Kenya Posta boss Dan Kagwe.
